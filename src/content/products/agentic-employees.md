The model is straightforward to describe: you hire a named AI agent for a functional role the way you'd hire a junior person. A defined remit. Defined tool access. Defined working hours. A weekly digest that says what the agent did and why. Inbox triage, SEO monitoring, lead qualification, first-pass support, content research — each is a discrete role with a clear handoff back to a human for anything outside the scope.

Each "employee" has a set of allowed actions and a kill-switch any operator can pull at any time. Every action emits a human-readable rationale paragraph that lands in the agent's working log. The weekly review interface is the artifact you open once and read end-to-end: what the agent did, what it paged to a human, what it skipped because it was out of scope. The reliability claim is operational — agents that act inside their remit and surface everything else, not agents that improvise.

This is a Probe, and it sits deliberately next to the Delegate card. Agentic Employees is named-roles-as-software; Delegate is operations-as-outcomes. The demand signal across both cards is what tells us whether these are two products or one.
