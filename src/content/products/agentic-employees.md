The model is exactly what it sounds like: you hire an AI employee for a named function the way you'd hire a junior person. A defined remit — what they're responsible for and what they're not. Defined tool access — which systems they can write to and which they can only read. Defined working hours. A weekly digest that says what they did and why, and what they escalated to you.

Inbox triage, SEO monitoring, lead qualification, first-pass support, content research — each is a discrete function with a clear handoff back to a human for anything outside scope. Every action the employee takes emits a rationale paragraph to its working log. The kill switch is always accessible. The reliability constraint is the same one you'd apply to anyone new on the team: act inside your remit and surface everything else — don't improvise.

This is a Probe sitting next to the Delegate card. Agentic Employees is about filling a named role; Delegate is about clearing a backlog of outcomes. The demand signal across both cards tells us whether these are two products or one. If you'd hire a role-shaped employee for a specific function, tell us — and which role you'd hire first is the answer that matters most.
