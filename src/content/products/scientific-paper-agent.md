A research analyst on staff does the reading you don't have time to do. A new paper lands, and instead of adding it to the pile, you hand it off: what does this claim, what does it cite, and where does it disagree with what you already know? The scientific paper agent handles that loop — locate the paper, extract the structured claims, follow every citation back to its source, and surface a one-page brief with every claim linked to the paragraph it came from.

The reliability constraint is the same one you'd put on a junior analyst: don't report a citation you haven't actually read. Don't paraphrase a claim without having the original sentence available. If you can't find a source, say so explicitly rather than skipping it. The agent works to those constraints. No hallucinated DOIs, no confident summaries of papers it hasn't fetched.
