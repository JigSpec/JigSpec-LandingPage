A new compound, mechanism, or methodology lands in a paper, and the question is always the same: what does this claim, what does it cite, and where does it disagree with the last twelve papers on the same topic? The scientific paper agent reads the paper, extracts the structured claims, follows every citation back to its source, and lays the cross-references out so you can see at a glance which arguments are well-supported and which are floating on a single citation chain.

The loop has four steps: locate the paper (DOI, arXiv, PubMed), extract the claims, cross-reference each citation against the bibliography graph the agent maintains for your research domain, and produce a one-page summary with every claim linked back to the paragraph it was lifted from. The reliability claim is concrete: no citation is reported unless the agent has actually fetched and indexed the cited source. No claim is paraphrased without the original sentence available. If the agent can't find a citation, it says so.

This is a Probe — the card exists because we think the problem is real, not because the product is built.
