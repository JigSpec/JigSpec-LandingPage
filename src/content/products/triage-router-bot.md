[Body placeholder — support inboxes drown the small ops shops that staff them. Lead-qualification forms drown founders doing their own sales. Customer-feedback channels drown product teams that asked for the feedback in the first place. The pattern is the same: messages come in faster than humans can read them, and the only way through is either to answer them slowly or to stop reading them carefully. A triage + router bot is what closes the gap.]

[The agent's loop is short: read each new message, classify it against a small set of routes the team configured up front, enrich it with whatever the team's tools already know about the sender (CRM record, prior tickets, account tier), and route it to the right human, the right tool, or the right canned reply. The reliability claim has three parts. First, no auto-reply goes out without a confidence threshold the team chose; below that threshold the message lands in a "needs a human" queue with the agent's reasoning attached. Second, every routing decision is logged to a tabular audit view the team can scroll through on a Friday afternoon and read end-to-end. Third, the escalation path is always one click from the triage page — there is no "stuck in the bot" failure mode.]

[Audience: founders running their own support, ops teams of three to ten people, agencies handling intake for multiple clients.
