An inbox manager on staff reads every message before you do. Support requests, lead-qualification responses, customer feedback, internal escalations — they get classified against the routes you defined, enriched with what your tools already know about the sender, and routed to the right place before you arrive. You open the queue and see what actually needs you, not what came in.

The reliability constraint is the same one you'd put on a new hire: don't send a reply you're not confident in. The agent has a confidence threshold you set; anything below it lands in a "needs a human" queue with the classification reasoning attached, so you can see why it wasn't sure. Every routing decision is logged to an audit view you can scroll through end-to-end. Escalation is always one click from the triage page — there's no "stuck in the bot" failure mode.

This is a Probe. If you manage your own support inbox, run intake for multiple clients, or qualify leads yourself, tell us — and the tool you'd route into (Zendesk, Linear, a Gmail label, a Slack channel) is the part of the answer that determines whether we build it next.
